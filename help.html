❓ FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How do I set up a custom domain?+
Go to Settings → Domains → Add Domain. Enter your domain name and follow the DNS configuration instructions. You'll need to add MX, SPF, and DKIM records. Setup typically takes 5-10 minutes.
Can I migrate from Gmail or Outlook?+
Yes! We offer one-click migration from Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo, and any IMAP-compatible provider. Go to Settings → Import → Select Provider and follow the wizard. Your folder structure, labels, and attachments are preserved.
Is my email really encrypted?+
Absolutely. We use end-to-end encryption (E2EE) based on the OpenPGP standard. Your emails are encrypted on your device and can only be decrypted by the intended recipient. We never have access to your encryption keys.
Can I cancel my subscription anytime?+
Yes, you can cancel at any time with no penalties. Your account remains active until the end of your current billing period. You can also downgrade to our free plan at any time.
Do you offer discounts for nonprofits?+
Yes! We offer 50% off all paid plans for verified nonprofit organizations and educational institutions. Contact our sales team with your organization details to apply.
What happens to my data if I delete my account?+
When you delete your account, all your data is permanently removed from our servers within 30 days. We provide a 30-day grace period during which you can recover your account. After 30 days, data cannot be recovered.
How do I enable two-factor authentication?+
Go to Settings → Security → Two-Factor Authentication. You can choose between TOTP (authenticator app), SMS, or hardware security keys (YubiKey). We recommend using an authenticator app or hardware key for the best security.
Can I use ThePost with my existing email client?+
Yes! ThePost supports IMAP and SMTP protocols, so you can use it with Thunderbird, Apple Mail, Outlook, and other email clients. Go to Settings → Email Client Setup to get your IMAP/SMTP credentials.

How to set up a custom domain

Updated: May 1, 2026 • 5 min read

Setting up a custom domain with ThePost is straightforward and takes just a few minutes. Follow these steps to connect your domain and start sending emails from your own address.

Step 1: Add your domain

Log in to your ThePost account and navigate to Settings → Domains → Add Domain. Enter your domain name (e.g., company.com) and click "Add Domain".

Step 2: Configure DNS records

You'll need to add three DNS records to your domain registrar:

  • MX Record: Points to mx.thepost.space with priority 10
  • SPF Record: v=spf1 include:thepost.space ~all
  • DKIM Record: Provided in your domain settings page
DNS propagation can take up to 24 hours, but typically completes within 5-10 minutes. You can check the status in your domain settings.

Step 3: Verify and activate

Once your DNS records are configured, click "Verify" in the domain settings. ThePost will automatically check your records and activate your domain. You'll receive a confirmation email once the domain is ready.
